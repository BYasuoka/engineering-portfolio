Mechanical Design Project

Automatic Watch Winder

Based on the current project folder, this case study centers on a compact CAD concept for a personal automatic watch winder. The available artifact is a rendered model, so this page focuses on the mechanical design intent visible in that geometry and the next engineering steps it suggests.

Design Analysis

Engineering readout from the existing model

Rotating cradle

The cylindrical upper body suggests a rotating watch support sized to keep the watch centered while minimizing side-to-side motion. Rounded surfaces also reduce visual bulk and are friendlier to fabrication via printing or smooth surfacing operations.

Motor and drive enclosure

The vertical side housing reads like a natural packaging zone for a motor, reduction drive, controller, and power routing. That separation between rotating volume and drive volume is a sensible layout choice for maintenance and assembly.

Support and stability

The wide lower base and integrated upright imply attention to tipping resistance and off-axis loading. For a consumer object that cycles for long periods, that stability is just as important as the motorized motion itself.

Manufacturability

Large fillets and simple planar side faces reduce stress concentrations and make the design easier to prototype. The geometry looks especially well suited to 3D printing for early testing before refining tolerances for a final build.

Key Design Questions

  • What torque and rotation schedule are required to keep a watch wound without overstressing the mechanism?
  • How will the watch be retained in the cradle while accommodating different case sizes and band stiffness?
  • Where should access panels, fasteners, and cable routing sit so assembly and maintenance stay simple?
  • How much sound and vibration damping is needed for a device intended to sit on a desk or dresser?
